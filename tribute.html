Biography: The King Of Pop

Born on August 29, 1958, from parents Katherine and Joe Jackson in Gary, Ind, Michael Jackson was a best-selling American singer, songwriter, and dancer. Michael Jackson was part of a big family with his six siblins Rebbie, Jackie, Tito, Jermaine, LaToya and Marlon. He was not only a solo singer but, when he was still a child, Michael Jackson had become the lead singer of his family's popular group, Jackson 5, where he was a lead singer in most of their songs. He's a 13-time grammy award winner and sold out many hit albums, his album thriller remaining the most famous. Michael Jackson had two spouses, Lisa Marie Presley and Debbie Rowe with a total of 3 children. On June 25, 2009, Michael J Jackson had died from cardiac arrest in Los Angeles, California, at age 50. It was proved that his death was caused by a combination of sedatives and propofol, which is an anesthetic. This was leathel and was then issused out as a homicide. In 2011, Jacksons personal doctor, Conrad Murray was reported guilty for involuntary manslaughter. Though Michael Jackson had died in 2009, his fame still lives on till this day.

Timeline: Here's The Timeline Of Michael Jackson

  • 1958: Michael Joseph Jackson is born to a family of eight with 6 older siblings, Rebbie, Jackie, Tito, Jermaine, LaToya and Marlon.
  • 1962: Michael and his siblings form a band which was previously not approved by their father, but then later he'd changed his mind and managed the group.
  • 1969: The song "I Want You Back" is a number-one single. "ABC (1970)," "The Love You Save" and "I'll Be There" follow suit.
  • 1971-1972: Michael Jackson becomes a solo singer. Many of his singles like "Got to Be There," "Rockin' Robin" and "I Wanna Be Where You Are" hit the charts.
  • 1978: Michael Jackson makes a film debut in The Wiz a retelling of the film The Wizard Of Oz.
  • 1979: Michael Jackson records his first album as a solo artist, Off The Wall. His singles "Don't Stop 'Til You Get Enough" and "Rock With You" become number one hits.
  • 1980: Michael Jackson gets his first grammy as the best R&B male vocal performance.
  • 1982-1983: Jacksons biggest hit album Thriller is released hiting the charts for 37 weeks. Seven of his singles hit top 10, including "Billie Jean," "Beat It," "Thriller" and "Wanna Be Startin' Somethin'." During these years, Michael Jackson invented his signature dance move, the moonwalk.
  • 1984: Jackson builds a home in Central California calling it Neverland with its own amusement park rides and covering 2,700 acres of land.
  • 1987: Jackson goes on a world tour after his third album Bad releases.
  • 1990: Michael Jacksons Thriller hits platinum for the 21st time, and the Guinness Book of World Records calls it the best-selling album ever. It has sold 65 million copies to this day.
  • 1992: Jackson reveals he has vitiligo, a skin disorder that can lead to skin cancer in severe cases. He also revealed that his father emotionally abused him.
  • 1993: Michael was accused of molesting an 11-year-old boy in civil court.
  • 1994: Jackson settles the molestation case out of court, and Jackson and Lisa-Marie Presley get married, but it only lasts for less than two years.
  • 1995: Janet Jackson and Michael Jackson perform a duet on Scream
  • 1997: Jackson marries his second wife, Debbie Rowe who gives birth to their son Prince Jackson. Michael Jackson is also introduced in the rock and roll hall of fame.
  • 1998: Jackson and his wife have a girl, Paris Michael Katherine.
  • 2000: Many of Michael Jacksons songs like "Billie Jean," "Rock With You," "I Want You Back" and "Beat It" make the top 100 list of greatest songs of all time for Rolling Stones magazine.
  • 2003: Michael Jackson is charged with seven counts of child sexual abuse and two counts of administering an intoxicating agent.
  • 2005: Jackson is acquitted on all previous counts from the year 2003.
  • 2006: Closure on the main house on the Neverland Ranch are forced due to financial struggles. Jackson also accepts eight records from the Guinness World Records in London during this year, and gets shared custody of his first two children.
  • 2007: Jackson and Sony buy Famous Music LLC from Viacom, giving him rights to songs by Eminem, Shakira, Beck and others.
  • 2008: Jackson celebrates 25 years of his iconic album Thriller. Sony also releases a fan-curated compilation named King Of Pop
  • 2009: Michael Jackson dies from cardiac arrest at the age of 50 in Los Angeles.
